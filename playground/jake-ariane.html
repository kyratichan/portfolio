Jake & Ariane's Wedding

Celebrating Filipino-Chinese heritage through a cohesive, backyard-inspired identity

Project Type

Freelance

Industry

Lifestyle & Events

Timeline

Aug 2023 - Apr 2024

Role

Event Branding, Web Design, Illustration

For my sister Ariane and her partner Jake, the vision was a "Filipino backyard" celebration that felt intimate, welcoming, and culturally resonant. To honor our shared Filipino-Chinese heritage, I worked closely with them to weave traditional motifs into a modern brand identity, designing everything from the tactile stationery to a dedicated wedding website to create a warm, cohesive invitation into their story.

The Vision

Creating a moodboard for inspiration

Event Branding

Creating a cohesive brand identity

To capture the 'Filipino backyard' vision, I curated a visual language that felt both cozy and culturally resonant. I selected Maragsa Display as the primary typeface, a choice inspired by the sharp, rhythmic strokes of traditional Filipino calligraphy. As a secondary typeface, I chose Mont Blanc to balance the design with a modern sans-serif that provided a clean, legible contrast. To complement the event’s rattan textures and tropical greenery, the color palette features warm earth tones that evoke the sun-drenched, lush landscapes of the Philippines.

Design & Validation

-----

To translate the product goals into a tangible solution, I began by conceptualizing the design through initial wireframes. These early artifacts served to facilitate conversations and quickly align cross-functional teams on a shared high-level vision, culminating in a successful early feature demo with a potential customer agency.. The validation received from this demo then propelled the process into rapid prototyping using Appian’s low-code SAIL framework, finalizing the design structure based on real-world feedback.

Impact

Feature release and results

Delivered as part of Appian’s Contract Writing solution, this feature was launched across multiple releases between April and September 2024. The successful rollout and design effectiveness were publicly validated by an official demo at the Appian World 2024 conference, where it received overwhelmingly positive feedback from various customer agencies.

The most critical validation came directly from users, underscoring the feature's immediate value:

This is so much better than what we use now! With our current system, PRISM, there’s no way of distinguishing between OTs and standard FAR contracts, so this would definitely give us a lot more flexibility.

This feedback confirmed the design's strategic goal: providing the flexibility and differentiation required to manage OTs effectively.

Future Considerations

Looking ahead at the roadmap

With the minimum viable product receiving such positive feedback, the next phase would focus on capabilities that can further enhance the value of our OT management solution to address customer needs, such as:

1

Complex Agreement Management

Add parent-child relationship functionality to allow funding limits on parent agreements to automatically cascade to child agreements, ensuring enhanced financial allocation control

2

Template Customization with a Terms & Conditions Library

Provide greater flexibility for agreement text customization by implementing a Terms and Conditions library. Users can quickly add pre-approved contractual articles, balancing rapid drafting speed with tailored contract requirements.

This would ensure the solution evolves to handle the most complex agreements, solidifying its role as the authoritative, flexible system for end-to-end OT management.
