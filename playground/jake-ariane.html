Jake & Ariane's Wedding

Celebrating Filipino-Chinese heritage through a cohesive, backyard-inspired identity

Project Type

Freelance

Industry

Lifestyle & Events

Timeline

Aug 2023 - Apr 2024

Role

Event Branding, Web Design, Illustration

For my sister Ariane and her partner Jake, the vision was a "Filipino backyard" celebration that felt intimate, welcoming, and culturally resonant. To honor our shared Filipino-Chinese heritage, I worked closely with them to weave traditional motifs into a modern brand identity, designing everything from the tactile stationery to a dedicated wedding website to create a warm, cohesive invitation into their story.

The Vision

Creating a moodboard for inspiration

Before diving into the deliverables, I collaborated closely with Jake and Ariane to establish a solid visual foundation, using an inspiration moodboard to align on typography, color themes, and a custom illustration style. Since the couple had already decided on a booklet format for their invitations, the moodboard focused on establishing a minimalist line-art aesthetic. We drew heavy inspiration from the delicate embroidery found on modern Filipino barongs, allowing us to translate traditional cultural motifs into a clean, contemporary graphic style that would carry across both print and digital platforms.

Inspiration moodboard

Event Branding

Creating a cohesive brand identity

To capture the "Filipino backyard" vision, I curated a visual language that felt both cozy and culturally resonant. I selected Maragsa Display as the primary typeface, a choice inspired by the sharp, rhythmic strokes of traditional Filipino calligraphy. As a secondary typeface, I chose Mont Blanc to balance the design with a modern sans-serif that provided a clean, legible contrast.
