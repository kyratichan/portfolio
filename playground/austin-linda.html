Austin and Linda's Wedding

Translating a love for nature into a refined, harmonious wedding identity

Project Type

Freelance

Industry

Lifestyle & Events

Timeline

Mar 2023 - Jul 2023

Role

Event Branding, Illustration

For my brother Austin and his wife Linda’s wedding, the goal was to create a visual identity as organic and serene as their garden venue. Drawing inspiration from their shared love of nature, I worked closely with them to design a cohesive branding system and a tactile invitation suite. This refined aesthetic provided a creative foundation, setting a tranquil and elegant tone for the entire celebration.

The Vision

Cultivating a natural and harmonious aesthetic

Austin and Linda initially began their design process with a local print studio, where they had compiled an inspiration moodboard to guide a first draft. However, knowing my background in freelance design, they decided to have me take over the project to create something more personalized. Drawing from their initial concepts, we collaborated closely to refine their vision into a fully realized identity, emphasizing the specific natural elements that were most meaningful to them.

To ensure the branding felt like a natural extension of the physical space, we drew direct inspiration from the wedding venue’s styling, which featured grand, flowing drapery. This architectural detail became a key visual anchor, leading us to explore tactile techniques like debossing to mimic those soft, wave-like textures on paper. By building on these early concepts, we established a design direction that was both structurally elegant and deeply reflective of their personalities.

Early moodboard inspiration alongside the studio's first design iteration

Event Branding

Defining a modern, botanical identity

For Austin and Linda’s brand identity, I leaned into a contemporary, minimalist aesthetic that balanced high-end elegance with organic warmth. I selected The Seasons as the primary typeface to establish a formal and timeless tone, pairing it with Nord Book for secondary headers. The clean contrast between typefaces kept the overall look feeling refined and effortless. The color palette was rooted in the couple's favorite floral tones and the venue’s lush surroundings. We chose Mauve as the anchor to match the bridesmaid attire, layering in Willow and Juniper to echo the greenery of the space. A mix of Ivory and Stone neutrals grounded these colors, creating an inviting and warm atmosphere. Central to the branding was the custom wedding logo, which featured a minimalist orchid — Austin and Linda’s favorite flower. As the centerpiece of the design, it served as a meaningful symbol of elegance and grace that tied the entire visual identity together.

Wedding Invitations

Crafting a tactile, personal invitation suite

Austin and Linda’s wedding invitation was designed as a tri-fold booklet, featuring an inner pocket to house individual cards for the entourage, attire, registry, and RSVP details. To echo the venue’s flowing drapery, I integrated wavy, debossed textures throughout the invitation, adding a tactile dimension to the paper. At the couple's request, we sprinkled the design with subtle nature motifs, such as a lilypad and koi fish, to evoke a sense of tranquility that complemented the botanical color palette.

Beyond the stationery, the brand identity extended into the event space itself. The custom wedding logo was transformed into a vibrant neon sign, serving as a focal point for the reception and a popular backdrop for guests.
