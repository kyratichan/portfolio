Role

Brand Identity, Art Direction, Packaging, Web Design

Bellevue is a wellness brand with a mission to simplify chronic pain relief through natural, movement-based solutions. While their first product, the Portable Back Stretcher, was a success, the brand lacked a visual identity beyond basic utility. Launching the Foot Essentials Kit became the catalyst to evolve Bellevue into a cohesive lifestyle brand, requiring a design system that could bridge the gap between the Amazon marketplace and a new, dedicated e-commerce presence.

The Challenge

Establishing a visual brand identity and ecommerce website for a health and wellness company

To leverage emerging technologies with greater speed and flexibility than standard Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) based contracts, government agencies use Other Transaction (OT) agreements to contract with non-traditional government contractors, such as research institutions, commercial companies, and tech startups. Although OTs bypass the rigid constraints of traditional FAR-based contracts, they also vary widely in structure and aren't manageable by legacy contract systems. Thus, integrating OTs as a core functionality within our Contract Writing solution is critical to address this management gap and significantly expand our addressable market.

Ideation

Creating a moodboard of illustration styles and other websites for inspiration

With Bellevue being in the health and wellness space, I wanted to use a minimalist style with a natural toned color scheme to match its values. Since their first product and initial branding followed a grayscale palette, I kept dark gray and white as the main brand colors for consistency. However, when it came to selecting the accent and secondary colors, I chose [color] and [color] to highlight [reason]. Next, to highlight body movement and activity within our illustration style, we were inspired by modern brands that used character illustrations with exaggerated limbs and an overall more flowy style, such as Outdoor Voices and Billie.

Product Goals

Enabling speed, flexibility and compliance

1

Seamless End-to-End Agreement Management

Enable rapid innovation by providing a seamless workflow that allows teams to instantly create, track, finalize, approve, and release all transaction agreements

2

Ensure Consistency with Template-Driven Agreements

Drastically reduce drafting time and eliminate errors by leveraging preset agreement text templates and logic-based auto-population capabilities

3

Centralize Performer Data

Mitigate compliance risks and streamline contract referencing by centralizing vendor, consortium and institution data in a unified system

4

Ensure Financial Accuracy Through Line Item Funding

Track and allocate funds at the line item level to minimize budget errors and eliminate overspending risk

Design & Validation

-----

To translate the product goals into a tangible solution, I began by conceptualizing the design through initial wireframes. These early artifacts served to facilitate conversations and quickly align cross-functional teams on a shared high-level vision, culminating in a successful early feature demo with a potential customer agency.. The validation received from this demo then propelled the process into rapid prototyping using Appian’s low-code SAIL framework, finalizing the design structure based on real-world feedback.

Impact

Feature release and results

Delivered as part of Appian’s Contract Writing solution, this feature was launched across multiple releases between April and September 2024. The successful rollout and design effectiveness were publicly validated by an official demo at the Appian World 2024 conference, where it received overwhelmingly positive feedback from various customer agencies.

The most critical validation came directly from users, underscoring the feature's immediate value:

This is so much better than what we use now! With our current system, PRISM, there’s no way of distinguishing between OTs and standard FAR contracts, so this would definitely give us a lot more flexibility.

This feedback confirmed the design's strategic goal: providing the flexibility and differentiation required to manage OTs effectively.

Future Considerations

Looking ahead at the roadmap

With the minimum viable product receiving such positive feedback, the next phase would focus on capabilities that can further enhance the value of our OT management solution to address customer needs, such as:

1

Complex Agreement Management

Add parent-child relationship functionality to allow funding limits on parent agreements to automatically cascade to child agreements, ensuring enhanced financial allocation control

2

Template Customization with a Terms & Conditions Library

Provide greater flexibility for agreement text customization by implementing a Terms and Conditions library. Users can quickly add pre-approved contractual articles, balancing rapid drafting speed with tailored contract requirements.

This would ensure the solution evolves to handle the most complex agreements, solidifying its role as the authoritative, flexible system for end-to-end OT management.
