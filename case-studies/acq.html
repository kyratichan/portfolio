The Appian Government Acquisition Management (GAM) suite provides specialized automation tools to streamline the federal procurement lifecycle. While these modular solutions offer deep functionality, the resulting fragmentation created information silos and "prioritization paralysis" for the contracting workforce. I spearheaded the product vision and proof of concept for the Acquisition Central hub, a centralized "command center" designed to consolidate disparate data and accelerate time-to-action for multi-solution customers.

The Challenge

Transforming fragmented tools to focused workflows

Federal contracting professionals operate in a high-stakes environment where critical data is often scattered across multiple, disconnected modules. This fragmentation forces users to spend their mornings manually orienting themselves, toggling between applications just to piece together task statuses and upcoming milestones. Without a unified view, time-sensitive requirements are often obscured by routine administrative noise, leading to "prioritization paralysis" and increased operational risk.

We designed a centralized "command center" to serve as the definitive starting point for the acquisition workforce. By consolidating critical actions into a single interface, the hub shifts the user experience from manual data-hunting to strategic action — enabling contracting personnel to focus on high-level procurement strategy rather than administrative navigation.

Research and Analysis

Grounding user frustrations within a validated market demand

To define our user requirements, we conducted internal stakeholder interviews and synthesized customer feedback. This research revealed that while their day-to-day tasks differ, every role shared a common frustration: a high cognitive load caused by systemic fragmentation.

Primary User Personas: The Execution Team

  • Contracting Officer (CO): Focuses on risk mitigation and compliance; hampered by process fragmentation and manual tracking
  • Contract Specialist (CS): Focuses on minimizing cycle times; slowed by navigational friction and complex policy interpretation

Secondary User Personas: The Oversight Team

  • Program Manager: Monitors contract performance; needs to bypass administrative bottlenecks to see procurement status
  • Business Admin: Manages agency communications; needs a centralized channel for system updates and data management

Proven Customer Demand

Our discovery process included an audit of custom-configured landing pages developed by major customers such as NNSA and Texas DPS. These agencies had already invested resources into building internal "splash pages" to solve for fragmented communication and data visibility. Seeing these agency-led initiatives confirmed a proven market value for a native, out-of-the-box hub that could centralize announcements, prioritized tasking, and graphical summaries—removing the need for costly custom development.

Designing the Solution

Bridging complex data with intuitive workflows

With the information architecture and visual brand defined, I translated these foundations into a system of high-density interfaces covering every stage of the acquisition process from solicitation to award. Each workflow was designed to consolidate massive procurement datasets into actionable views, streamlining the review process and increasing transparency across teams. This approach was validated by strong client interest and feedback, confirming the solution’s ability to turn fragmented tasks into a unified experience.
