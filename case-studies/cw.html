I led the User Experience (UX) efforts for the Contract Writing solution within the Government Acquisition solution suite from its 1.0 to 2.0 release.

The Challenge

Streamlining management to accelerate procurement cycles

Government contracting is critical to any agency mission, but acquisition professionals are often hindered by outdated and inefficient systems, overwhelming amounts of structured data, input from disparate sources, limited process control, and inadequate visibility. Built on the Appian Platform, our Government Acquisition solutions offer agencies end-to-end visibility into the acquisition process paired with industry-leading workflow capabilities, dramatically improving process efficiency throughout the acquisition lifecycle. These modular applications can be fully integrated to provide a complete end-to-end solution or implemented as standalone solutions. When I first joined Appian in August 2022, I was tasked with designing Contract Writing, our latest solution within the suite that focuses on automating solicitation and award creation to simplify contract management.

Research and Analysis

Translating user priorities into a scalable structural foundation

Since the industry and requirements for the contract writing solution were already established, I focused on reading documentation about public sector use cases for my initial ramp-up. From these resources, I refined the user personas and information architecture.


Core Personas

Requestor / Program Office

  • Responsible for defining a detailed scope of goods and services needed, drafting all required documents, and setting a realistic budget and timeline expectations

Contracting Personnel

  • Responsible for ensuring that the government is making an informed buying decision and negotiating a fair, equitable and beneficial contract within a realistic timeline
  • Process submitted requirements and requisitions into solicitations and/or awards, including amendments and modifications
  • Sign awards and warrant amounts prior to signatures

Information Architecture

While the high-level site architecture was largely established, the detail views for Solicitations and Awards required a more granular focus. The original architecture for these views was too flat, overwhelming users with an endless row of tabs and duplicative data. To improve discoverability, I facilitated a card sorting exercise with my product manager and stakeholders to inform a sitemap overhaul. We replaced the clutter with a balanced hierarchy and sub-navigation, helping users better contextualize information and orient themselves within complex datasets.

Defining the Visual Brand

Modernizing the Government Acquisitions suite

As one of Appian’s foundational offerings, the Government Acquisitions suite carried a legacy aesthetic that felt outdated compared to modern standards. With Contract Writing serving as the suite’s flagship product, we leveraged this redesign to establish a new visual North Star—positioning the suite as a key market differentiator. The Solutions UX team introduced a contemporary design system, anchored by a sophisticated color palette of nine core colors across six saturations, balanced by neutral blue-gray tones for a professional, high-density interface.

Designing the Solution

Bridging complex data with intuitive workflows

With the information architecture and visual brand defined, I translated these foundations into a system of high-density interfaces covering every stage of the acquisition process from solicitation to award. Each workflow was designed to consolidate massive procurement datasets into actionable views, streamlining the review process and increasing transparency across teams. This approach was validated by strong client interest and feedback, confirming the solution’s ability to turn fragmented tasks into a unified experience.
