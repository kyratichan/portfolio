Appian is a low-code platform that focuses on creating and deploying workflow automation applications. The Solutions team within Appian extends the low-code platform to offer out-of-the-box solutions for horizontal use cases and vertical industries. I led the User Experience (UX) efforts for the Contract Writing solution within the Government Acquisition solution suite from its 1.0 to 2.0 release.

The Challenge

Simplifying contract management and reducing procurement action lead time (PALT)

Government contracting is critical to any agency mission, but acquisition professionals are often hindered by outdated and inefficient systems, overwhelming amounts of structured data, input from disparate sources, limited process control, and inadequate visibility. Built on the Appian Platform, our Government Acquisition solutions offer agencies end-to-end visibility into the acquisition process paired with industry-leading workflow capabilities, dramatically improving process efficiency throughout the acquisition lifecycle. These modular applications can be fully integrated to provide a complete end-to-end solution or implemented as standalone solutions. When I first joined Appian in August 2022, I was tasked with designing Contract Writing, our latest solution within the suite that focuses on automating solicitation and award creation to simplify contract management.

Research and Analysis

Understanding contracting personnel priorities and defining the underlying structure

Since the industry and requirements for the contract writing solution was already established, I focused on reading documentation about public sector use cases for my initial ramp-up. From these resources, I refined the user personas and information architecture.


Core Personas

Requisitioner / Program Office / Contracting Officer Representatives (COR)

  • Responsible for defining a detailed scope of goods and services needed, drafting all required documents, and setting a realistic budget and timeline expectations

Contracting Officers (CO) / Contracting Specialists (CS)

  • Responsible for ensuring that the government is making an informed buying decision and negotiating a fair, equitable and beneficial contract within a realistic timeline
  • Process submitted requirements and requisitions into solicitations and/or awards, including amendments and modifications
  • Sign awards and warrant amounts prior to signatures

Information Architecture

While the general site architecture was already somewhat established, the architecture of the detail views for each Solicitation and Award required more attention.

The original information architectures for our record views were based on what we had for other solutions within the suite offering and did not follow UX best practices as it was too flat, leading to an overload of tabs that made it difficult to scan and sections that seemed duplicative. I worked with my product manager and stakeholders to propose an alternative sitemap to improve discoverability –– one with a more balanced hierarchy and intermediate sub-levels to help the user contextualize the information in each section.

Designing the Solution

Modernizing the Government Acquisitions suite

Because the government acquisitions suite was one of the first Appian solution offerings, most of the other solutions had a very outdated look and feel. With Contract Writing being the core product of the solution suite, we wanted to use this opportunity to push for a modern redesign that would serve as a key market differentiator for Appian. The Solutions UX team deliberately selected a contemporary color palette that consisted of eight colors in four saturations, along with four blue-gray neutral tones.
